Mac Basics

Everything you need to get started.

Apps

Work, create, connect

Your Mac comes with built-in apps like Safari, Mail, and Calendar — to help you be productive and stay connected. To open an app, click its icon in the Dock.

Finder

See your files

The Finder is the place to access all of your files. Click a button in the toolbar to choose how you want to view them — as icons, in a list, in columns, or in Gallery View.

Spotlight

Search your Mac

Click Spotlight button in the top-right corner of your screen, or press Command ⌘ + Space bar, to easily search files and apps on your Mac. When you find what you’re looking for, press Return to open it.

Keyboard Shortcuts

Save time

Quickly perform actions with just your keyboard. To copy an item, press the Command ⌘ and C keys. The Command key is frequently used in shortcuts, as are Shift ⇧, Control ⌃, and Option ⌥.

Notifications

Get a heads-up

Stay on top of upcoming calendar events, new messages, and incoming emails. You can accept an invitation or reply to a text right from the notification.

Safari

Browse the web

Safari is the fastest and most secure way to surf the web on all your Apple devices. Use the Smart Search field to search the web — start typing and Safari suggests news, websites, and more.

Messages

Text from your Mac

With Messages, you can send a text without reaching for your phone. Choose a contact, type your message, then press Return to send. To share more, like a photo or Memoji, click App Store icon .

Photos

Organize and edit

Scroll to see your photos organized by date and location. Click in the sidebar to see your photos grouped in other ways — by Memories, People or Places. Double-click a photo to take a closer look.

Mail

Manage your email

Keep all of your email accounts in the same place — with a separate inbox for each account. Add your accounts to the Mail app by choosing Mail > Add Account. To view messages from a single account, click its name in the sidebar.

Entertainment

Listen, watch, enjoy

Your music, movies, TV shows, and podcasts are organized into three apps — Apple Music, Apple TV, and Apple Podcasts. Watch and listen to your favorites, or discover something new chosen just for you.

Apple ID

All about you

Manage everything connected to your Apple ID in one place — including your password, payment information, devices, and iCloud settings. Just open System Preferences and click Apple ID.

Continuity

Start here, finish there

With Continuity, you can seamlessly continue what you’re doing as you move from one device to another. For example, open a message on your iPhone, then use Handoff to reply on your Mac.
